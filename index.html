KEY CONTEXT ON MARGIN COMPRESSION
  • Overall margin % compressed from 4.84% → 2.61% — intentional trade-off: competitive rates offered to win seller trust and expand supply 5×.
  • Patti modification (payment slips adjusted 50–100 Rs in specs) was a major source of seller friction last season. This season it was reduced to rare instances — foundational to relationship building.
  • Removing a single anomalous collection from last year's data, the true baseline was ~4%, making real compression ~140 bps, not 220 bps.
  • Feb 26 was the season peak at ₹7.32 Cr GMV — Feb + Mar together = 56% of season topline.

WHAT CHANGED IN SUPPLY — KEY DRIVERS
  • Better rates to sellers — made us more competitive vs mandi traders and direct buyers.
  • Same-day Patti (immediate payment acknowledgement) — key trust-building lever; most sellers previously waited days.
  • 70% advance per dispatch — per-vehicle advance on every consignment, reducing seller cash flow risk significantly.
  • Access to new markets — Chennai, Madurai, Hyderabad; sellers could not independently access these high-realisation markets.
  • Two of three sourcing managers hired from Vegrow — their network and category expertise contributed ~50% of season volumes.
05 · ACM — ACTIVE COMMISSION MANAGEMENT OBJECTIVE: SELLER RETENTION VIA PRICE REALISATION
What is ACM?  Active Commission Management = Ninjacart's team actively sells 20% of stock in each vehicle in destination markets, generating direct price realisation data and preventing DTs from low-balling pattis. These trades earn 5.69% margin — 2× of normal DMP commission trades.

WHAT WORKED
  • Identification of the correct players and markets for ACM activation.
  • CHs became well-versed in quality, specs, and market dynamics — reduced DT leverage.
  • DTs could no longer take us for granted — indirectly reduced delinquency.
  • Bengaluru (forwarding market) and Chennai (underwritten buyers) — easiest ACM execution.
WHAT DID NOT WORK
  • Regular 20% stock sale was operationally draining for CH / ZH teams.
  • SR Fruits (biggest partner) refused ACM interference in vehicle sales. MNR consistently blocked access.
  • Burned ₹35 L in Oct/Nov testing Order Mode purchase + Commission Mode sale — not viable in current buy-sell ecosystem.
ACM — WAY FORWARD FRAMEWORK
TRIGGER MECHANISM
  • Central team maintains Patti Rating per seller across markets
  • If rating falls below threshold → ACM activated for that market for 1 month
  • Track Seller Satisfaction + Patti score improvement
USE CASE (NOT SCALE PLAY)
  • New markets — use ACM to understand SKU + identify right DTs
  • New categories — train team via ACM before full DMP roll-out
  • 20% target on every vehicle is not the goal anymore
EXECUTION PROTOCOL
  • Central team gives expected price inputs per vehicle before arrival
  • Ground team sells / shares feedback post-vehicle arrival
  • Outcomes tracked vs vendor and market score
