⚠ Critical Finding — Structural Productivity Crisis

HP apple production has declined every year from 3.58 crore boxes in 2019-20 to ~2.08 crore (2024-25) and ~2.0 crore (2025-26) — despite area under cultivation remaining stable at ~1.16 lakh ha. This is a yield and productivity crisis, not an area problem. The 2026-27 season is projected at 1.0–1.4 crore boxes — the lowest in at least a decade.

Key Production Belts — Shimla District

Kinnaur is a separate district — not part of Shimla. It produces premium Fuji, Gala, and Kinnour Red from September onwards at 8,000–11,000 ft altitudes, commanding a separate market premium.

Crop Conditions
Weather & Bloom Damage Assessment
Phase 1 · Oct–Feb
Winter Chilling Deficit

Below-average snowfall across Shimla, Kullu, Mandi. Warmer-than-normal dormancy window → insufficient chilling hours at 5,000–8,000 ft. Royal Delicious requires the highest chilling hours of any commercial variety — most vulnerable.

REQUIRED: 1,000–1,600 hrs <7°C · SHORTFALL: Significant
Phase 2 · Late Feb–March
Premature Bud-Break

10°C+ temperature spike triggered bud-break 2–3 weeks ahead of normal across low and mid-elevation orchards. Open blossoms left exposed before protective canopy development — highly vulnerable to subsequent cold events.

ADVANCE: 2–3 weeks early · RISK: Frost exposure at bloom
Phase 3 · April 2026
Multi-Event Bloom Damage

Major storm: rain + hail + dust simultaneously. Heavy dust on blossoms in Baghi-Ratnari and Kotkhai disrupted bee activity and pollination. Frost above 7,000 ft burned flowers and young fruitlets. Storm physically damaged flower clusters across Kotkhai, Jubbal, Narkanda.

TRIPLE IMPACT: Dust · Frost · Physical Damage
⚠ Crop Risk Summary — Season 2026-27

Fruit Growers Association of HP (FVFGA) estimates crop loss up to 70% in major apple belts. Low and mid-elevation regions face 60–80% fruit-set reduction. At time of this report (May 2026), the primary assessment concern is fruit-set failure — how many fruitlets have formed per tree. Fruit development and sizing assessment will follow in June–July as the season progresses. High-altitude belts and Kinnaur district (separate from Shimla) report more moderate impact due to later bloom timing.

This is the largest primary field survey the company has conducted to date — ground-level signal significantly more granular than official HP Horticulture Department estimates.

Key Field Observations

Baghi-Ratnari is the most severely impacted pocket — fruit-set estimated at only 30–40% of normal. Hatu Range (8,000–9,000 ft) is the relative outlier at ~26% decline due to altitude-buffered later bloom timing. Anti-hail nets (~20–25% of farms) offered moderate protection against hail but none against frost or pollination disruption — the dominant damage mechanisms this season. Gala's proportional share of surviving fruit will be higher than normal, with early arrivals expected from mid-July from Mandi transition and lower Kullu farms.

Strategic Conclusions
Recommendations & Actions
C1 — Season Outlook

Sharply below the weak 2025-26 base. 1.0–1.4 crore boxes forecast — lowest in a decade. Royal Delicious (70–80% of Shimla) absorbed the most damage. Primary concern at this stage (May 2026) is fruit-set failure, not downstream quality or sizing.

C2 — Import Opportunity

Open, wide, and well-timed. Gap of 0.8–1.0 crore boxes vs prior year, plus J&K headwinds. Delayed domestic season extends the uncontested import window. Red Delicious and Royal Gala are priority varieties.

C3 — Structural Insight

New baseline is 1.5–2.0 crore boxes, not 3.5 crore. Build multi-year import procurement models around the structural new HP output reality. This is a productivity crisis on stable area — unlikely to self-correct quickly.

Action Plan — Next 30–60 Days

1
Fix Red Delicious import contracts immediately
Iran and Turkey origins. Target July/August 2026 landing. This is the highest-volume, most direct substitute for the domestically scarce Royal Delicious. Do not wait for July mandi prices to confirm the shortfall — it will be too late.
2
Confirm NZ Royal Gala orders for September landing
Premium channel positioning. Domestic Gala down 25–40% — NZ Gala fills the gap. Freight cut-off from NZ is ~20–22 days; order window closes mid-June.
3
Secure cold chain slots across key hubs — by end-June
Higher import volumes will pressure Delhi/NCR cold storage capacity. Secure in advance. Do not leave this until July arrivals create a scramble.
4
Set up weekly HP mandi price tracker from July 1
Monitor Shimla, Solan, and Kullu APMCs. Use live price data to dynamically manage import release timing and channel pricing. This is the early-warning system for any domestic supply surprise.
5
Monitor HP Horticulture Dept. official production estimates
Official estimates expected July 2026. Historical pattern: initial estimates are consistently optimistic (2024-25 forecast was 2.91 cr; actual was 2.08 cr). Discount official estimates by 20–30% based on our field data.
6
Revisit farms in July for fruit-sizing stage assessment
Current data reflects fruit-set only (May 2026). June–July assessment will reveal sizing, final volumes, and any recovery signals from high-altitude orchards. Refine volume estimates before peak procurement decisions.
7
Rebuild multi-year import models around 1.5–2.0 crore box HP baseline
The old 3.5+ crore box "normal" is no longer the planning basis. HP production has structurally declined — area is stable but yield has halved since 2010-11. Build procurement strategy around ~1.5–2.0 crore boxes as the new HP annual expectation.
